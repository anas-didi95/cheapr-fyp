Introducing Cheapr

Cheapr is an application about assisting consumers to locate products, to predict prices of product accross different stores.The prediction is made on weekly basis.

Comprehensive data

Data from catalogues are updated every week

We are very concern with the data new prices that the supermarkets sell their products. We carefully insert the information such as name, price, day/month/year, and location of the products. It is our responsibilities in making sure our users will always updated with recent list of pricing released by the supermarkets.

We also provide six categories for users to ease the utilization of our system instead of using searching feature. The categories such snack, drink, ingredient, fresh food, household, and toiletries are very comprehensive for all kinds of user and family-friendly to use. Plus, attractive interface will also delight our users mood while using our application.

One product will never be enough

Add items as much as you please, as long as you got the money!

We provide the user a feature called 'virtual cart' which allow them to add the items that they want into the cart and help them to manage their shopping. The idea of virtual cart is to assist the users to manage their shopping and track the products that they want to buy. This feature is very useful as the users are very pasrticular with the items that they want to buy so the budget of their shopping would not exceeded.

Bare in mind virtual cart only helps users to keep on track with their shopping not to buy and pay them via online transiction. Our system does not collaborate with any financial company to provide users with the transaction. Therefore, they have to go to the supermarkets and pay them phsically.

Forecasting weather is 'so yesterday'

Predict an upcoming prices using machine learning's module

As we know, technologies these days are currently implementing an artificial intelligence in devices to allow machines to learn new and predict events based on previous data given. We use this idea to help users in predicting the prices of products and who knows it might save their money a little bit. We feed our module with the data we manually insert and let the machine do their job on guessing the price that might the supermarkets release next week.

With a big help from Microsoft Azure, we are able to predict the prices of the products more than 70% of accuracy everytime the machine produce the outputs. The Neural Network's algorithm is the best algoritm that we use to to help our machine learning module and your financial planning.

Vizualize the data

See ups and downs of the prices that you want

Cheapr also has a business-related faeture which allows users to view the statistics or graph that shows the the fluctuation of the prices. This feature helps users to see whether the prices are becoming higher or lower based on the previous data that we store in our database.

Some users might use this feature to analyse and create their own assumptions and predictions. Users will find this useful as a tool to view the trends of market prices based on the factors such as current currency, festivas, and seasons. Experts know how to use this tool much more than that, as long as see how the prices are going throughout the weeks.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this application can be used for every supermarkets?

This application is still under development, the supermarkets that we provide for now are Econsave, Aeon, and Giant only. Other than that is not available.

What payment methods do you accept?

The feature that we implemented which is virtual cart only applicable for user to add their desired products into one collectable place only, the same as bookmarks. We do not provide any online transaction for this system.

Do I have to register to use this system?

No, user can straightly use the system in smartphone and computer without having to register to this system. This allow the user to take a quick glance at the prices and ease the uses for every level of community.

Why some of the products prices are not same as the markets?

The methodology of collecting the data that we use for this system is by key-in one by one the information from the catalouge that we found online. To avoid outliers and accuracy of the data, we modify some of the name and prices in order to robust the system.
